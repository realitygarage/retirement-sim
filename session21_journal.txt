--- 1. ENGINE ROUTING CHANGE (engine.js + App.jsx wfData mirror) ---

Removed hiPaydownPct entirely (SC_DEFAULTS/DEFAULTS in defaults.js, the sc
destructure/setter/slider in App.jsx, the paydownByYear tracking in engine.js).
The old mechanism split the post-draw remainder into a "dedicated paydown budget"
(hiPaydownPct% avalanche) THEN a second "remainder" avalanche against the SAME
debts -- mathematically redundant (both stages hit the same debts in the same
avalanche order; splitting them only changed which bucket a dollar was logged
under). New mechanism: splitResidual() is called without paydownPct/totalDebt
(paydownBudget resolves to 0, remainder = pool - draw), and the FULL remainder
goes through ONE planHiPaydown() avalanche call.

Annual engine (already effectively debt-first pre-change, just needlessly split):
draw -> avalanche(full remainder) -> mortgage-principal bucket -> sweep savings.
wfDebtByYear now holds the TOTAL debt paydown (was previously just the "extra"
second-stage amount); hiPaydown/paydownByYear fields removed from row output.

Monthly engine (genuine behavior change, confirmed with user): was
draw -> dedicated-avalanche(paydownBudget) -> fill reserve buckets to caps with
remainder -> leftover joins the regular monthly sweep-or-savings decision. Now:
draw -> avalanche(FULL remainder) -> fill reserve buckets with whatever's left ->
leftover joins sweep-or-savings. This reorder is SCOPED to the one-time sale-year
inflow block only; the ordinary recurring monthly waterfall (Fixed Costs ->
Maintenance Reserves -> Rainy Day/Op Buffer -> FCF Floor -> Surplus sweep) is
UNCHANGED. New row fields: oneTimePaydown, oneTimeReserveFill (alongside the
existing oneTimeSweep/settleDraw).

Added settleDrawLabel (optional free-text, default '') alongside the existing
settleLifestyleDraw $ amount -- "lifestyle vs. other use" per spec §4. Cosmetic
only, doesn't touch engine math.

New conservation invariant: draw + debt-first paydown + reserve-fills + savings
=== pool (was: draw + dedicated-paydown + waterfall-debt + waterfall-savings ===
residual). Verified live (R8: pool=$777,587, debtPaid=$259,174 fully clears
launch-balance HI debt when the sale happens in the obligation's own year 2026;
R11: selling in 2030 instead -- after ~4yr of ordinary avalanche has already
reduced the balance below $259,174 -- shows paydown correctly tracks the ACTUAL
balance at sale time ($179,738), fully zeroes hiDebt, THEN $58,505 flows to
reserve buckets. This is the debt-first-then-buffers proof.)

kept UNCHANGED (explicitly, per user): payOffHI ("HI Debt at Closing: Sweep over
time / Pay off at closing" toggle -- a separate v3.2 global "debt already paid
off" assumption used in ~10 places, not a duplicate of the routing chain); the
Cash Flow tab's lifestyleSplit ("% of surplus above floor to keep" sweep dial) --
the ongoing monthly rate lever, untouched.

--- BUGS CAUGHT DURING VERIFICATION ---

My own first draft of R11 asserted paydown ≈ $259,174 (the default LAUNCH HI-debt
balance). Failed: actual paydown was $179,738. Root cause -- selling in 2030
(deliberately chosen to dodge the 2026 obligation) also gives ~4 years of the
ORDINARY monthly avalanche sweep a head start, so the balance AT the sale month is
already below the launch figure. Rewrote to assert against hiDebtAfter===0 (the
real invariant -- debt fully cleared, not "cleared by a specific dollar amount")
instead of hardcoding a balance that drifts with unrelated sweep dynamics.

Test-runner tooling glitch (not app-related): run_tests.ps1's Start-Transcript
mechanism failed twice in a row to capture the headed Playwright run's output --
the transcript showed only a stray "check on the tests" line and a truncated
timestamp gap matching the real ~6-7min run duration, with no actual test output
captured. Worked around by invoking `npx playwright test --headed --reporter=list`
directly (bypassing the transcript wrapper) and reading the tool's own captured
stdout -- this worked cleanly across three separate runs. Flagging for whoever
picks up next: run_tests.ps1's transcript capture may not be reliable in this
environment for long headed runs; the direct npx invocation is the more trustworthy
fallback if run_tests.ps1's own transcript looks suspiciously short/garbled again.

--- SEMANTIC NOTES ---

- The pooled-routing chain is now strictly: proceeds -> obligation -> one-time
  draw -> dispersement cascade (debt-first, then reserves/buffers, then savings).
  There is no longer any "% of pool to debt" dial -- the cascade always sends
  every dollar of the post-draw remainder against HI debt first, by construction,
  in both engines.
- payOffHI is a DIFFERENT, unrelated lever (global "debt already paid off at
  launch" assumption) and was never part of this redesign.
- The monthly engine's one-time-sale-inflow debt-first reorder does NOT change
  the ordinary month-to-month waterfall (still Fixed Costs -> Maintenance
  Reserves -> Rainy Day/Op Buffer -> FCF Floor -> Surplus sweep, buffers before
  the debt sweep, as designed) -- the reorder is scoped narrowly to the one-time
  lump sum a property sale generates.
- The separate Cash Flow tab (Tier 1-5 waterfall editor) is functionally
  UNTOUCHED this session and remains a distinct, non-duplicating supplemental
  detail screen per the user's explicit direction.
