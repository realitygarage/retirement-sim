=== SESSION 28 JOURNAL — v4.3.1 (6th St Selling-Cost Double-Count Fix) ===

Date: 2026-07-09

Session goal: user flagged a calibration bug via the agent's real net sheet --
6th St's disposition tax math was double-counting selling costs, understating
taxable gain ($175K sim vs $275,450 CPA-correct). Asked for investigate-
first-report-a-plan (no code) before touching anything: confirm whether all
three properties' basis defaults already capitalize selling costs, and if
so recommend the cleanest fix across all three, showing before/after
numbers, before implementing anything.

--- INVESTIGATION (before any code changed) ---

Confirmed the double-count is REAL, but only for 6th St. Found the smoking
gun in `v3_phase1_consolidated_spec.md` (the original v3.1.0 design spec),
an inline comment at the exact basis line:

    adjustedBasis: 899_550,  // $735K cost + $76K improvements + $88.55K closing

Exact match to the user's $88,550 figure. 15th St and Barberry have NO such
itemization anywhere in the codebase -- just "carryover after 1031, per
sheet." That's not incidental: both were acquired via a 1031 exchange from
Bonair, so their basis is a carryover basis from that original transaction
-- mechanically it wouldn't normally include costs of a FUTURE anticipated
sale of Barberry/15th themselves.

Ran disposeAsset directly (not hand math) via a throwaway probe.spec.js
(deleted before commit) to test candidate fixes against each property's
stored cpaEstTax/cpaNetProceedsAfterTax:

6th St: current sim (6% selling cost applied on top of cost-inclusive
basis) gives gain $174,950 / net $719,669 vs CPA $275,450 gain / $708,881
net. Naively zeroing sellingCostsPct fixes the gain exactly ($275,450) but
OVERSTATES net proceeds by $83K ($791,828) -- because netSale was doing
double duty: used both for the gain calc (should exclude selling costs,
since basis already has them) AND for the cash net-proceeds calc (should
still deduct the real ~5% cash cost of selling, which is a real outflow
regardless of basis treatment). A structural split -- gain uses gross price
verbatim, net proceeds separately deducts a real ~5% -- reconciles gain to
$275,450 exactly and net to within $803 (0.1%) of CPA's $708,881.

15th St / Barberry: tested the SAME "remove costs, apply real %" treatment
using the user's stated embedded amounts ($72,750 / $34,050) -- it made
BOTH properties' CPA reconciliation WORSE, not better, in every variant
tested (current unchanged was closest to CPA every time). This is strong,
reproducible evidence the fix does not apply to these two -- consistent
with (1) above (no documented cost-inclusive basis, and a 1031 carryover
basis mechanically shouldn't have one).

User confirmed: proceed with the structural split for 6th St only; leave
15th/Barberry untouched.

--- WHAT SHIPPED (v4.3.1) ---

engine.js:
- `disposeAsset`: added `prop.sellingCostsInBasis` (per-property, defaults
  falsy/false -- NOT a global toggle). `netSale` (= grossPrice - sellingCosts)
  keeps its existing meaning UNCHANGED -- it's the CASH side, used for
  afterTaxNetProceeds and partial_1031's freedEquity, and a dev-mode audit
  in App.jsx already asserts netSale === grossPrice - sellingCosts, so its
  semantics couldn't be repurposed without breaking that check. New
  `gainBasisPrice` variable is the GAIN/TAX side: grossPrice verbatim when
  sellingCostsInBasis is set (the cost is already embedded in the basis
  input), else netSale as before (unchanged path for every other property).
  `realizedGain` now derives from gainBasisPrice instead of netSale --
  this one line change is the entire fix; everything downstream (§121,
  sell/full_1031/partial_1031 branches) already consumed realizedGain, so
  no other logic needed to change.
- `computeDispo` (inside buildScenario): passes `sellingCostsInBasis:
  hold.sellingCostsInBasis || false` into disposeAsset's prop object, and
  `sellingCostsPct: hold.sellingCostsPct` into its opts (undefined for
  every property except 6th St, so disposeAsset's existing `opts.sellingCostsPct
  ?? cfg.sellingCostsPct` fallback keeps the global DISPO_DEFAULTS.sellingCostsPct
  (6%, UNCHANGED) for 15th/Barberry -- the 6%->5% real-rate correction is
  scoped to 6th St only, not a global default change, precisely so 15th/
  Barberry stay byte-identical).

defaults.js:
- 6th St's `hold` block: added `sellingCostsInBasis: true` and
  `sellingCostsPct: 0.05` (the real agent-net-sheet rate: 2x2.5% commission
  + title + closing, ~$89K on $1.675M), both with comments explaining WHY
  and explicitly noting 15th/Barberry do NOT get this treatment and why.
  `basis: 899_550` itself is UNCHANGED (still cost-inclusive, per the
  original spec) -- the fix is entirely in how disposeAsset now reads it,
  not in the basis number itself.

Result (verified end-to-end via buildScenario+dispoResults, obligation

--- WHY 15th St / Barberry ARE EXCLUDED (for future sessions) ---

Do NOT apply sellingCostsInBasis (or a basis reduction + rate correction)
to 15th St or Barberry without new information. Both are 1031-carryover
basis properties (from the Bonair exchange) with:
  1. No documented selling-cost component in adjustedBasis anywhere in this
     codebase's history (v3_phase1_consolidated_spec.md's comments for
     these two just say "carryover after 1031, per sheet" -- no itemized
     $ breakdown, unlike 6th St's explicit "$735K + $76K + $88.55K").
  2. Numerically, EVERY variant of "remove costs from basis" tested this
     session moved their CPA reconciliation WORSE (further from CPA), not
     better -- the opposite of what happened for 6th St, where the fix
     landed within $803 of the CPA net figure.
If a future session gets NEW primary-source numbers (the actual Ottinger.xlsx
CPA worksheet, not just the codebase's historical comments) showing these
two DO have an itemized selling-cost component in their carryover basis,
that would be grounds to revisit -- but absent that, leave them alone.
